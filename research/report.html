Design Research вҖ” HushBook landing page

How top mobile-app landing pages handle dual iOS+Android download CTAs, single-page feature showcases, and TOS / Privacy / About вҖ” with real references. Compiled 4 Jun 2026 В· Lazyweb screenshot DB + web research.

TL;DR. The winning pattern is a single scrolling page: a hero that proves the product + a clear download CTA, then social proof, a feature-card showcase with app screenshots, a repeated/sticky download bar, and a footer linking Privacy / Terms / About. HushBook's edge is the hero itself вҖ” no audiobook competitor uses kinetic typography, so a scroll-driven "karaoke" hero (oversized words that highlight word-by-word and sweep leftвҶ”right) is a genuine differentiator. Pair it with the table-stakes structure below and lead the Privacy page with the on-device story.

Recommendations do this

  1. Lead with a kinetic "karaoke" hero that demonstrates the core feature. Most app pages show a phone mockup; HushBook can instead be the feature вҖ” oversized book text that lights up word-by-word as you scroll. It's the X100 detail competitors don't have. Keep it readable and add a reduced-motion fallback.
  2. Put download front-and-centre, then repeat it. One CTA above the fold, official App Store + Google Play badges in a dedicated section, and a sticky mobile download bar. (authy, transit, gamecharger, beeper.)
  3. One scrolling page: hero вҶ’ meaning вҶ’ feature cards вҶ’ app screenshots вҶ’ providers/social proof вҶ’ download вҶ’ footer. Feature-card grids with screenshots + an FAQ are the universal shape (onX, ultrahuman, Apple Books/Audible).
  4. Sell Privacy on "on-device," and make Terms handle user imports. Lead the Privacy page with "nothing leaves your phone"; Terms must cover imported-audio rights + a DMCA path (Instapaper-style tabs, tripsy app-privacy layout).
  5. Footer carries the legal + about links (Privacy, Terms, About, contact) вҖ” the standard home for them (selleb, photomath).

Patterns (table stakes)

  • Hero = value prop + one CTA, ideally with product proof (mockup, demo, or вҖ” for us вҖ” the live feature).
  • Social proof early: ratings, user counts, "as seen in" logos, awards (gamecharger, ultrahuman, transit).
  • Feature cards with real screenshots, often a 2вҖ“3 column grid (onX, Apple Books, Apple Card).
  • Cross-device / "listen everywhere" row is standard for audio apps (Audible, Apple Books).
  • FAQ accordion near the bottom (Apple Books, Audible, Everand, ultrahuman, musi).
  • QR code to bridge desktopвҶ’mobile install (transit, gamecharger, gemini, Calm).
  • Official store badges + a repeated/sticky CTA; legal links live in the footer.

Anti-patterns (avoid)

  • Restyling/animating the official store badges вҖ” Apple & Google both forbid it; keep Вј-height clear space, min ~40px.
  • Burying the download with no sticky/repeat CTA on mobile.
  • Walls of legal text with no in-page TOC or section anchors.
  • Generic stock hero that shows nothing of the product (every strong example proves the app).
  • Kinetic type that hurts readability or ignores prefers-reduced-motion вҖ” motion must enhance, never block the words (especially for an accessibility-first app).

Unique angles (steal these)

  • Speechify / Speech Central foreground word-highlighting and accessibility/vision profiles вҖ” exactly HushBook's strengths; we can out-do them by making the hero itself the karaoke demo.
  • Art of Fauna proves big high-contrast scrolling text on dark reads beautifully вҖ” validates the hero treatment.
  • neuecast shows a hero can be a single phone-player mockup + one button; restraint is on-brand.
  • Everand positions via a comparison table ("vs Audible") вҖ” a later option for HushBook ("vs plain audiobooks").
  • traffic.productions (the user's reference): oversized editorial type that travels leftвҶ”right on scroll вҖ” the kinetic spine of our hero.

Findings

The Lazyweb corpus is strong for the buckets that matter most here: audiobook/audio competitors (Apple Books appears across many sites; Audible, Everand, Speechify, Speech Central, neuecast), hero-with-dual-download-buttons, single-page feature showcases, and legal/about pages. It is thin on true kinetic-typography heroes вҖ” the closest were brand/marketing pages (HubSpot Spotlight, Deeo) and type-specimen experiments rather than app landing pages. That gap is the opportunity: a scroll-driven karaoke hero would stand out in the audiobook category, where pages are screenshot-and-FAQ formulaic.

Build implication, carried into the prototype: reuse the app's own visual language вҖ” dark lyrics canvas (#0B0B0B, blurred ambient backdrop), word spoken/idle colours, brown accent (#6F3825 / #C98A6A) вҖ” so the site feels like the app, and let GSAP ScrollTrigger map scroll position to "playback."
